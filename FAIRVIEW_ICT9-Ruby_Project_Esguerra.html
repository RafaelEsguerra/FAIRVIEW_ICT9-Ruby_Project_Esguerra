Fire is very quick! A little flame can become a large conflagration in less than 30 seconds. A house might be filled with heavy black smoke or can catch fire in a matter of minutes.

Fires are very hot! Flames are less dangerous than heat. In a fire, room temperatures can range from 100 degrees at eye level to 600 degrees at floor level. The intense heat of this air will burn your lungs and melt your clothing to your skin.

Dark is the fire! Fire begins off brightly but shortly turns into total darkness and black smoke.

Fire can be fatal! More people are killed by smoke and hazardous gases than by flames. Gases produced by fire are toxic and cause drowsiness and confusion.



How to Prevent Fire?

Examine your house		 To make sure that it is completely inspected, you might need to recruit—or perhaps hire—someone with experience in home electrical wiring, plumbing (gas), heating, and air conditioning.

Examine the state of the electrical system in your house.		 To prevent an electric shock, put on rubber sandals and hold a wooden stick or other object in your hand. To be safe, take off any jewelry or metallic accessories you may be wearing because they could carry electricity.

Check your home's natural gas or LP gas system.		 In the vicinity of these appliances, keep an eye out for any loose fittings, leaking valves, malfunctioning pilot lights, debris, or carelessly stored combustible materials.

Verify your home's heating and cooling systems.		 These systems use electric motors and air-moving machinery, both of which need routine maintenance. To remedy any problems with your electrical devices, especially air conditioners, always work with a reputable professional.
